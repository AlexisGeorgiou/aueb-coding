Nikon D3400
Nikon packs a lot of very desirable features into the D3400 for the price.
The D3400 left a bit to be desired considering that this is supposed to be an updated
version of the D3300. With the removal of the in-body sensor cleaning and the external mic port
we expected there to be a few more updates to the D3400 and, well, we just didn't get them.
Although it still is one of the best entry-level DSLRs out there especially
considering its price point.

Nikon D750
There's no such thing as the perfect camera, but Nikon's D750 is damn close.
The D750 goes from basic to highly advanced... It is up to you to decide what you want from the camera.
The photos are mouth watering. Accurate colors have a depth you just do not
get with less-expensive APS-C sensors. Video capture is quite good and the movies are among the best
we have ever shot with a DSLR.

Canon EOS 1300D
Not everyone needs an all-singing and -dancing camera, and if the 1300D was straightforward and effective
I would happily give it a big thumbs up. However, there are a few traits that are actively frustrating. With the Canon EOS 750D currently available for around 450 euros, it is worth paying the extra for its articulated touchscreen, superior autofocus for both photos and video,
faster continuous performance, larger viewfinder and various other upgrades. Meanwhile, those on tighter budgets are better served
by the Nikon D3400, which is still available, a more dependable camera and is currently available for around 300 euros with its kit lens.
As with the 1200D before it, the Canon 1300D fails to rise above its rivals and siblings.

Canon EOS 750D
The Canon Rebel is arguably one of the most, if not the most, recognisable brand in the camera industry to many consumers, and Canon will probably sell boatloads of T6i no matter what we write in this review. The camera is an evolutionary follow-up to the T5i, adding a higher resolution sensor and improved autofocus, but with no significant changes in physical design. Its image quality is on par with most of its peers and the camera is capable of creating beautiful photos. Dynamic range performance lags behind the state of the art sensors that Sony offers; this will likely grate at enthusiast photographers, but whether this difference will matter to the majority of Rebel buyers is debatable. For someone who wants nice photos of a vacation or family event, and who plans to shoot mostly in Jpeg, there is a lot to like.
